The Myth

"Mangoes should be made illegal because they give people diabetes."

This claim is false. No single fruit causes diabetes. Type 2 diabetes develops from a combination of factors including genetics, overall diet quality, physical activity level, and body composition over time.

Key Facts

  • Mangoes contain natural sugars along with fiber, vitamins (especially vitamin C & A precursors), and polyphenols.
  • Fiber and polyphenols can help moderate post-meal blood glucose responses.
  • Portion size matters: a typical serving is about 3/4 to 1 cup (100‚Äì150 g).
  • Whole fruits are linked with lower risk of type 2 diabetes when part of balanced eating patterns.
  • Replacing ultra-processed sweets with whole fruit generally improves diet quality.

Enjoy Mangoes Smartly

  1. Pair with protein (e.g., Greek yogurt) or healthy fats (e.g., nuts) to slow glucose absorption.
  2. Use mango to sweeten smoothies instead of added sugars.
  3. Balance your plate: half non-starchy veggies, a quarter lean protein, a quarter whole grains or starchy carbs, plus fruit.
